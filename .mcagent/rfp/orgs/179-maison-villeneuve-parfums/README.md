**"Four generations of jasmine. One family. No shortcuts."**

## Heritage

Maison Villeneuve was established in 1923 by Auguste Villeneuve, a jasmine grower in Grasse who decided to stop selling his harvest to the large fragrance houses and start making perfume himself. He built a small extraction facility next to his jasmine fields and produced the house's first fragrance — "Jasmin de Grasse N.1" — from his own crop. The fragrance was sold locally. It was adequate.

His daughter, Marguerite Villeneuve, was not interested in adequacy. She trained at ISIPCA in the 1950s, returned to Grasse, and reformulated every fragrance in the house's catalog. She introduced modern extraction techniques (solvent extraction, then supercritical CO2) while insisting on sourcing jasmine exclusively from the family's fields. Under her direction, Maison Villeneuve became known for a single thing: the finest jasmine absolute in Grasse. The house produces 12 fragrances. Every one contains jasmine.

Marguerite's son, Jean-Pierre Villeneuve, expanded the business cautiously through the 1990s and 2000s, adding export markets in Japan and the US. He is 71, semi-retired, and still walks the jasmine fields every morning at 5 AM to check the blossoms. He does not fully understand what AI agents are. He has been told they will help. He is reserving judgment.

Jean-Pierre's daughter, Eloise Villeneuve, joined the house in 2016 after an MBA at INSEAD and three years at L'Oreal. She is 34, impatient with tradition when it slows the business, and respectful of tradition when it defines quality. She introduced e-commerce, social media marketing, and computational analysis of the house's jasmine absolute — GC-MS characterization that her grandfather would have considered sacrilegious and her father considers unnecessary.

## Tension

**The Jasmine Question.** Jean-Pierre insists that every Maison Villeneuve fragrance must contain at least 15% jasmine absolute from the family's fields. Eloise argues that this constraint limits the house's creative range and drives up costs — jasmine absolute is one of the most expensive natural ingredients, and the family's fields produce a limited harvest. She wants to create a secondary line with reduced jasmine content, sold at a lower price point. Jean-Pierre considers this a betrayal of Auguste's vision. Marie-Claire, who has witnessed every Villeneuve family argument for 28 years, stays neutral. Hugo sides with Eloise. Fatima sides with Jean-Pierre. The secondary line has not been launched. The argument continues at every family dinner.

## Achievement

In 2023, Maison Villeneuve's jasmine absolute won the Prix d'Excellence at the International Raw Materials Competition in Grasse — the first time a single-estate producer had won in 15 years. The judges noted "exceptional complexity, with an indolic depth rarely achieved in modern jasmine extraction." Jean-Pierre accepted the award with a speech about his grandfather that made the audience cry and Eloise check her phone. Marie-Claire framed the certificate and hung it in the lab, between the GC chromatogram of the winning batch and a photograph of Auguste Villeneuve standing in the jasmine fields in 1925.
