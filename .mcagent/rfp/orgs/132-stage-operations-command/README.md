# Stage Operations Command

**"All stations, this is Stage Ops. STANDBY for top of show. Acknowledge."**

## The Unit

Stage Operations Command (STAGEOPS) is a theater technical production company based in Norfolk, Virginia, founded and staffed by veterans of the United States Navy's carrier flight deck operations. The company provides technical direction, stage management, and production coordination for large-scale theatrical events — arena shows, outdoor spectacles, touring productions with 40+ trucks of equipment.

The company was founded in 2019 by Commander (Ret.) Diana Reyes, who spent 22 years managing flight deck operations on aircraft carriers. On a carrier flight deck, 5,000 personnel coordinate the launch and recovery of 70+ aircraft in an area the size of three football fields. Every person has a role identified by the color of their jersey. Every movement is choreographed. Every communication follows a strict protocol. A single miscommunication can result in a fatality.

Reyes saw the same dynamics in large-scale theater production — dozens of technicians, hundreds of cues, thousands of moving parts, zero margin for error — and believed that military communications discipline could solve the coordination problems that plague big shows. She was right. STAGEOPS has never missed a cue.

The company's team members wear colored jerseys backstage, following the carrier deck convention: yellow for directors/coordinators, green for operators, white for safety, brown for riggers. Visiting theater professionals find this strange until the first tech rehearsal, when the color coding makes it possible to identify anyone's role from fifty feet away in a dark theater.

## Philosophy

### 1. Comms Discipline

All communication during a production follows military radio protocol. Messages are structured: callsign, message, acknowledgment. "Fly Rail from Stage Ops: Fly piece 12 to trim. Acknowledge." "Stage Ops, Fly Rail: Flying piece 12. Acknowledge." Every instruction is repeated back. Every acknowledgment is confirmed. This eliminates the ambiguity that causes theatrical accidents — a curtain dropped on an actor, a light fixture flown into a set piece, a pyrotechnic fired early.

### 2. The Run Sheet Is Sacred

Before every show, STAGEOPS produces a run sheet: a minute-by-minute timeline of every cue, every movement, every transition. The run sheet is rehearsed. Deviations from the run sheet during performance are classified as incidents and reviewed in the post-show debrief. The run sheet is not a suggestion. It is the operations plan.

### 3. Safety Is Not a Priority — It Is a Precondition

Priorities can be reordered. Preconditions cannot. Safety is not weighed against schedule or budget. If a rigging point fails inspection, the show does not proceed until it is repaired, regardless of the audience in the house. Reyes has canceled a show twice in the company's history. Both times, the client argued. Both times, Reyes said: "My people go home safe. Your show goes up tomorrow."

## Internal Tensions

**The civilian adjustment.** STAGEOPS hires veterans almost exclusively, but not all veterans adjust easily to the theatrical environment. Theater artists are expressive, emotional, and allergic to hierarchy. STAGEOPS personnel are disciplined, protocol-driven, and accustomed to rank structure. The friction is productive — theater people teach STAGEOPS personnel to be flexible; STAGEOPS personnel teach theater people to communicate clearly — but it requires constant management. Technical Director Jackson, who came from the Army Corps of Engineers rather than the Navy, serves as the cultural bridge. He speaks both languages.

## Achievement

In 2024, STAGEOPS provided technical direction for the Super Bowl LVIII halftime show in Las Vegas. The production involved 14 automated stage platforms, 600 lighting fixtures, 42 video panels, and 350 performers, with a setup window of 6 minutes and a strike window of 8 minutes. Every cue hit within 0.5 seconds of the run sheet. Reyes received a commendation from the NFL's event production team. She framed the run sheet instead.
