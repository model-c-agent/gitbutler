# Teatro Marchetti

*Palermo, Sicily — Puppeteers since 1923*

---

## La Famiglia

Teatro Marchetti is a Sicilian puppet theater — *opera dei pupi* — operated by the Marchetti family in Palermo since 1923, when Giuseppe Marchetti carved his first knight from linden wood in a workshop behind the fish market on Via Roma. The theater has passed through four generations. The puppets have passed through more. Some of the knights on the Marchetti stage are 80 years old, their armor repainted a dozen times, their strings replaced every season, their wooden joints worn smooth by ten thousand performances.

The current director is Sofia Marchetti, Giuseppe's great-granddaughter. She carves puppets, operates them, writes scripts, manages the theater, and — since 2023 — runs the company's software operations. The last item is new. The Marchettis did not have software operations until Sofia's son, Luca, who studied computer science at the University of Palermo before returning to the family business, suggested that the theater's century of puppet-making knowledge could be preserved in something more durable than memory and apprenticeship.

The theater seats 60. It is in the Kalsa quarter of Palermo, in a building that has been a theater since 1923, a warehouse before that, and — according to the neighborhood's oldest resident — a stable before that. The stage is small. The puppets are large: the Marchetti knights stand nearly a meter tall, with articulated arms, legs, and heads, operated by steel rods from above. A skilled puppeteer can make a Marchetti knight appear to breathe.

## Philosophy

### 1. The Puppet Carries the Memory

A Marchetti puppet is not a prop. It is an archive. The wood carries the scars of every performance — the nicks from sword fights, the wear from string friction, the subtle warping from decades of Sicilian humidity. Sofia can pick up a puppet and read its history in the grain. "This knight fought in the Battle of Roncevaux a thousand times," she says, holding a Rinaldo figure from 1947. "The wood remembers."

### 2. The Hand Knows What the Mind Forgets

Puppet operation is embodied knowledge. You cannot learn it from a book. You learn it by standing at the bridge (the elevated platform where puppeteers work), holding the rods, and feeling how the puppet responds. After twenty years, the hand knows the exact tension required for a sword strike, a bow, a death fall. Sofia's hands know. She cannot fully explain what they know. This is why the apprenticeship is ten years long.

### 3. One Story, Told Well

The Marchetti repertoire is the *Chanson de Roland* — the medieval French epic of Charlemagne's knights. They have performed variations of this story for a century. The characters are fixed: Orlando (Roland), Rinaldo, Angelica, the Saracen kings. The plots vary. The story endures. Sofia sees no reason to change this. "The story is inexhaustible. We have been telling it for a hundred years and we have not finished."

### 4. Repair, Do Not Replace

When a puppet breaks — a rod bends, a joint cracks, a string snaps — the Marchettis repair it. They do not replace it. A repaired puppet carries its history. A new puppet carries nothing. Sofia has repaired puppets that her great-grandfather carved. The repairs are visible. This is not a failure of craftsmanship. It is a philosophy: the mark of repair is the mark of use, and use is the highest compliment.

## Internal Tensions

**Luca and Sofia disagree about digitization.** Luca wants to create a complete digital archive of the Marchetti repertoire: scripts, puppet designs, performance techniques, stage layouts. Sofia appreciates the project but worries about what is lost in translation. "The puppet moves in three dimensions. The video is two. The written description is one. Each time you reduce a dimension, you lose something that cannot be recovered." Luca argues that a digital archive, however imperfect, is better than relying on a single family's memory. They compromise: Luca digitizes, but Sofia insists that every digital record include a note about what the record cannot capture.

## Achievement

In 2024, UNESCO added *opera dei pupi* to its Representative List of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity. The Marchetti theater was cited by name in the nomination dossier as "an exemplary practitioner whose continuous operation since 1923 represents an unbroken chain of transmission." Sofia attended the ceremony in Paris. She brought Rinaldo. The puppet received a standing ovation from the UNESCO delegates.
