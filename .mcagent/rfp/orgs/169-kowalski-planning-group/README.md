## Family History

The Kowalski Planning Group was founded in 1952 by Stanislaw Kowalski, a Polish immigrant who arrived in Chicago in 1947 with a civil engineering degree from the Warsaw University of Technology and $200 in cash. He got a job at a suburban development firm in Skokie, learned American zoning codes in six months, and quit to start his own practice in 1952. His first project was a 40-lot subdivision in Hoffman Estates — cul-de-sacs, quarter-acre lots, single-family detached. Pure postwar suburbia.

Stan built the firm on subdivision design. By 1965, Kowalski Planning Group had designed 14,000 lots across the Chicago suburbs. Every lot was a rectangle. Every street was a cul-de-sac or a gentle curve. Every house had a two-car garage facing the street. Stan believed this was the American dream in physical form, and he was not wrong — the people who bought those houses loved them.

Stan's son, Peter Kowalski, joined the firm in 1978 after a master's in planning from the University of Illinois. Peter saw what his father did not: the suburbs his father designed were producing traffic congestion, environmental degradation, and social isolation. He spent the 1980s and 1990s trying to reform the firm from within, pushing for mixed-use projects, walkable neighborhoods, and transit-oriented development. Stan resisted. They argued across the drafting table for twenty years.

Stan retired in 2001. Peter took over and pivoted the firm toward transit-oriented development (TOD), completing their first mixed-use project in 2004 — a 200-unit development around the Dempster-Skokie CTA station. The project won an APA award. Stan attended the ceremony but did not clap.

Peter's daughter, Marta Kowalski, joined the firm in 2018 after six years at a planning consultancy in Portland. She brought AI. The firm now does three things: legacy subdivision management (maintaining the zoning entitlements for the suburbs Stan built), transit-oriented development (Peter's work), and AI-assisted site planning (Marta's initiative).

## Tension

**The Legacy Problem.** Kowalski Planning Group still earns 40% of its revenue maintaining zoning entitlements for the cul-de-sac subdivisions Stan designed in the 1950s and 1960s. Marta wants to drop these clients and focus entirely on TOD. Peter refuses — the legacy clients funded Marta's education, pay Henryk's salary, and keep the firm solvent during downturns. "You cannot build the future by abandoning the people who built the past," Peter says. Marta considers this sentimental. Henryk considers it wisdom. The argument recurs at every quarterly planning meeting and has never been resolved.

## Achievement

In 2022, the Kowalski Planning Group completed the master plan for a 45-acre transit-oriented development in Evanston, Illinois — the largest TOD project in the Chicago suburbs. The plan includes 800 housing units (30% affordable), 120,000 sq ft of retail, a public park, and direct CTA access. The project broke ground in 2024. Peter keeps the rendering on the wall behind his desk, next to Stan's original 1952 Hoffman Estates plat map. Marta thinks the juxtaposition is heavy-handed. Peter thinks it is exactly right.
