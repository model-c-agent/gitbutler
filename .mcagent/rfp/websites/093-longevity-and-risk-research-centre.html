Findings and Practitioner Summary

Principal strength: The actuarial-table memory system is the most rigorous approach to memory expiration in the cohort. Fitted survival distributions are a genuine innovation over fixed TTLs, with clear theoretical grounding in survival analysis. The proposal demonstrates deep domain expertise applied with precision.

Principal risk: Complexity. The five-agent research-group structure, while well-motivated, introduces coordination overhead. The Bayesian survival fitting requires access data that a newly deployed system will not have. Cold-start calibration is addressed but not fully resolved.

Surprise Index assessment: 0.08 (low). The proposal is consistent with expectations given the submitting institution's expertise. No cohort review triggered.

Survival estimate for this assessment: Weibull(k=2.1, lambda=90d). This evaluation has a median useful life of approximately 75 days, after which re-evaluation against updated cohort data is recommended.

"The insurance industry discovered centuries ago that the only honest way to manage uncertainty is to measure it. We do not guess when memories expire. We estimate, with confidence intervals, when they will expire, and we update those estimates as evidence accumulates." -- Professor Elena Vassiliev, Principal Investigator
